How Technology Can Help You Grow Healthy Bonsai Plants

Bonsai is a art of growing trees in small containers. Firstly, it is necessary to think about the difference with “a potted plant” to define the bonsai.
Any plant becomes the potted plant if set into a pot. The important characteristic of the bonsai must not be able to greatly grow up idly. It is the volume of the root that decides the size of the tree. In the case of a bonsai, prevent the expansion of the root by putting it in the small pot daringly. And techniques such as pruning wiring branches and disbudding used to limit growth.
A basic idea of a bonsai is a replication of nature, in the form of a miniature tree set into a pot, mimic the shape and scale of full size trees. It is a culture producing scenery of nature on the pot. Bonsai is a collaboration between the vitality of trees and the skills of human beings.

Bonsai can be roughly divided into two groups which are Syouhaku Bonsai and Zouki Bonsai.



Syouhaku Bonsai


It is the generic name of the evergreen conifers such as a pine or the hinoki. These types of Bonsai are strong and durable, and branches are rarely broken; they are relatively easy to treat than other types of Bonsai.


Zouki Bonsai


It is the generic name of the deciduous tree. This type of Bonsai can enjoy the change of the four seasons including colored leaves and fallen leaves. It is relatively fast-growing, diligent care is necessary. There are genres called 'Hamono', 'Hnamono' and 'Mimono' in the Zouki Bonsai.


Hanamono


It is the generic name of the flowering tree such as a camellia or a cherry tree and the plum.


Hamono


This type of Bonsai has a beautiful leaf. Also, the coloured leaves are beautiful.


Mimono


This is the generic name of the fruit trees such as a pradu wood or the pomegranate tree.
